Wyoming leads anonymous incorporations in the US, OpenCorporates data shows

New Limited Liability Companies, preferred for their privacy and low taxation, reached a new high in Wyoming in 2023, surpassing Delaware, which had historically been on top. This type of company, traditionally suited for small businesses, has become a good fit for shell companies over the years.

2023: Wyoming outdoes Delaware in incorporations per capita

Last year, the Cowboy State recorded a historical 42% increase in incorporations, up to 378 per capita. In the rest of the states, only Montana has ever recorded over 50 incorporations per capita.

Registrations of new companies in Wyoming have tripled in the past five years, shows a recent analysis of OpenCorporates database.

The majority of these incorporations are Limited Liability Companies (LLC), which pay no taxes in Wyoming and are easy to set up from anywhere in the world. Currently, 75% of all companies registered in Wyoming are LLCs.

For many years, Delaware was the indisputable destination for new entities and it is still the domicile of over 67% of Fortune 500 companies, according to Delawares Division of Corporations.

But in 2023, Wyoming surpassed Delaware for the second time with 378 new companies per 1,000 adults. The average of new companies created last year in the rest of the US was 11, not including Puerto Rico.

While in Delaware, the growth of LLC run in parallel with the rise of other types of companies, in Wyoming this has not been the case.

Wyoming LLCs: an unparalleled rise in the last decade

Reasons behind this new trend can be traced by looking back at the timeline. Wyoming was, in fact, the birthplace of LLCs in 1977. This structure provided small business owners with personal liability protection while offering flexibility in management and taxation.

In the next decades, LLC became popular not just in Wyoming but across the country, and not just fo small businesses but for a wide range of purposes. In the aftermath of the Panama Papers, many included Delaware in the list of tax havens, typically located in small countries.

While LLC is not a synonym for dodgy business, its protection of secrecy makes it very difficult to track illicit activities. A disproportionate number of LLCs per adult capita can signal the presence of shell companies, according to the EU Tax Observatory, an independent research laboratory.

Shell companies have little or no operations in the place where they are officially located. Instead, they exist primarily as entities on paper, used for transactions and asset holding, generally to exploit regulations, create opaque ownership structures, or reduce tax obligations, says the research organisation.

While Wyoming has attracted new LLCs, other types of companies have not gained the same popularity and have remained at levels similar to the average of other states. This has led to an abnormally high number of LLCs per capita in Wyoming.

12 times more new Wyoming LLCs per capita compared to average

The latest changes in Wyoming's legislation, around 2010, enhanced privacy protections for LLC members and managers. They also allowed for the creation of multiple LLCs under a single entity.

2016 marked a turning point, when incorporations in Wyoming took off with an increase of nearly 31%. The year before, the Tax Foundation, a business-friendly think tank, ranked Wyoming at the top due to the state's lower taxes, a position it has maintained ever since. In Wyoming, LLCs are free of personal and corporate income tax, which in Delaware they are not.

These reforms coincided with the raising popularity of online company formation services, which enable people to establish LLCs in Wyoming from abroad.

While the advantages of Wyoming LLCs are tied to their operations within the state, in the digital world clients can be located anywhere. This allows e-commerce businesses, such as the $100,000 Donald Trump's watches, to deliver products worldwide. As long as they maintain their operations within Wyoming, they dont risk incurring fines.

Cryptocurrency and cyberattacks

Wyoming has been trying hard to become the crypto hub par excellence in the US, with the adoption of about 30 new crypto-friendly or crypto-permissive laws since 2019 and in March this year, the state passed a law for Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs), which sets them apart from other types of companies, such as LLCs. The state is also preparing to become the first state to have a state-backed cryptocurrency.

The ease and anonymity of Wyoming LLCs seem to be attracting cybercriminals as well. In September last year, the International Press Institute (IPI) website was taken down shortly after the organisation reported on how cyberattacks were threatening Hungarian independent outlets. Reuters linked this event, along with a handful other high-profile hacks, to Wyoming LLCs.

Singular locations

Sheridan is a small town four hours away from Mount Rushmore and home to about 19,000 people. Theres a place with this name in over twenty states, but only in Sheridan, Wyoming there are 320,308 LLCs registered. This means that there are 16 times more LLCs than residents of all ages.



Most LLCs in Sheridan, up to 96%, are registered at one of two addresses. Nothing in the appearance of the buildings suggests that they are home to thousands of companies.


Wyoming connection

If you buy one of Trumps watches, it will be shipped to you, if all goes well, from a company named TheBestWatchesonEarth LLC. This company, established by the end of July, is registered at one of the two popular addresses in Sheridan: 1309 Coffeen Avenue.

The organizer of this entity, according to OpenCorporates data, is Andrew Pierce. Formerly a Caribbean business developer, he is the founding partner, along with Mark Pierce, of Wyoming Trust and LLC Attorney, formally known as Cloud Peak Law Group, which is also registered at 1309 Coffeen Avenue. On their website, they briefly describe the history of the family business: What was the most difficult hurdle? Estate taxes and divorces.

This is not the first collaboration between Pierce and the former president. In February, they launched Trump Sneakers, licensed under the business name 45footwear LLC. Andrew Pierce is listed as the organizer, with Cloud Peak serving as the registered agent, similar to the arrangement for Trumps watches. The company was set up just some weeks before the sneakers were advertised. The sneakers, sold as a limited edition for $399 a pair, quickly sold out. Months later, 45footwear LLC filed a federal lawsuit against online merchants selling counterfeits and sought millions of dollars in damages.

Cloud Peak Law Group serves as the officer of 7,108 LLCs in Wyoming, according to OpenCorporates' database. This means that the company has facilitated the formation of that many entities. Interestingly, a company with the same name is also registered in New Castle, Delaware.

Impact in the US

Not only in Wyoming have LLCs outnumbered other company types, also when we look at overall numbers instead of per capita.

LLCs have overtaken other company types across the US

Summing up annual incorporations in each state, the proportion of new Limited Liability Companies has increased by 12% over the past decade.
